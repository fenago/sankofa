# LearnGraph Theory Overview

## Quick Reference Guide to 29 Educational Psychology Frameworks

This document provides a concise overview of all educational psychology frameworks implemented or under consideration in LearnGraph.

---

## Part 1: Content Analysis Frameworks (9 Implemented)

These frameworks are applied during source processing to build an educationally-grounded knowledge graph.

### 1. Bloom's Taxonomy (Revised 2001)
**Researcher:** Anderson & Krathwohl
**Core Concept:** 6 cognitive levels from simple to complex
- Remember → Understand → Apply → Analyze → Evaluate → Create
- **Application:** Every skill is classified by cognitive complexity level

### 2. Item Response Theory (3PL)
**Researcher:** Lord (1980)
**Core Concept:** Psychometric parameters for each item
- Difficulty (b), Discrimination (a), Guessing (c)
- **Application:** Predicts learner success probability at any ability level

### 3. Threshold Concepts
**Researcher:** Meyer & Land (2003)
**Core Concept:** Transformative, irreversible knowledge
- Gateway concepts that unlock entire domains
- **Application:** Identifies knowledge worth extra investment

### 4. Cognitive Load Theory
**Researcher:** Sweller (1988)
**Core Concept:** Working memory limits
- Element interactivity, chunking, split attention
- **Application:** Prevents overload; optimizes learning sequence

### 5. Instructional Scaffolding
**Researcher:** Vygotsky, Wood, Bruner, Ross (1976)
**Core Concept:** 4 support levels
- L1: Full worked examples
- L2: Partial solutions
- L3: Hints only
- L4: Independent practice
- **Application:** Systematic independence building

### 6. Mastery Learning
**Researcher:** Bloom (1968)
**Core Concept:** Clear mastery thresholds
- 80% for standard skills
- 90% for threshold concepts
- **Application:** Research-based definition of "learned"

### 7. Misconception Detection
**Researcher:** Cognitive Science
**Core Concept:** Common errors per skill
- Troublesome aspects, typical mistakes
- **Application:** Proactive error prevention and remediation

### 8. Spaced Repetition Intervals
**Researcher:** Wozniak (1987), Ebbinghaus (1885)
**Core Concept:** Optimal review spacing
- Default intervals: 1, 3, 7, 14, 30, 60 days
- **Application:** Maximizes retention; prevents decay

### 9. Prerequisite Relationship Mapping
**Researcher:** Learning Progression Research
**Core Concept:** Dependency relationships
- Required, Recommended, Helpful strength levels
- **Application:** Ensures proper learning sequence

## Part 2: Inverse Profiling Frameworks (12 Implemented)

These frameworks analyze learner behavior in real-time to personalize the experience.

### 10. Bayesian Knowledge Tracing (BKT)
**Researcher:** Corbett & Anderson (1995)
**Core Concept:** Probabilistic mastery estimation
- P(Mastery) with 4 parameters: pL0, pT, pS, pG
- **Application:** Real probabilistic mastery, not just % correct

### 11. Zone of Proximal Development (ZPD)
**Researcher:** Vygotsky (1978)
**Core Concept:** Readiness-based progression
- Skills where prerequisites are mastered
- **Application:** Always working in productive struggle zone

### 12. SM-2 Spaced Repetition
**Researcher:** Wozniak (1987)
**Core Concept:** Personalized intervals
- Ease factor adjusted by response quality
- **Application:** Personalized retention optimization

### 13. Dynamic Scaffold Adaptation
**Researcher:** Scaffolding Fading Research
**Core Concept:** Auto-adjusting support
- Level changes based on P(Mastery)
- **Application:** Right amount of help at all times

### 14. Metacognitive Calibration
**Researcher:** Zimmerman (2002)
**Core Concept:** Self-awareness measurement
- Confidence vs. actual performance
- **Application:** Reveals over/underconfidence

### 15. Help-Seeking Pattern Analysis
**Researcher:** Self-Regulated Learning Research
**Core Concept:** Hint usage patterns
- Avoidant (<10%), Appropriate (20-40%), Excessive (>50%)
- **Application:** Identifies unhealthy learning patterns

### 16. Goal Orientation Detection
**Researcher:** Dweck (2006), Nicholls (1984)
**Core Concept:** Motivation type
- Mastery vs. Performance vs. Avoidance
- **Application:** Adapts messaging and encouragement style

### 17. Working Memory Estimation
**Researcher:** Cognitive Load Theory, Miller's Law
**Core Concept:** Capacity inference
- From response times and error patterns
- **Application:** Adapts content complexity

### 18. Persistence & Engagement Tracking
**Researcher:** Engagement Research
**Core Concept:** Frustration detection
- Consecutive failures, session duration, skill avoidance
- **Application:** Early dropout prevention

### 19. Error Pattern Analysis
**Researcher:** Diagnostic Assessment Research
**Core Concept:** Systematic mistake detection
- Error types matched against known misconceptions
- **Application:** Distinguishes systematic vs. random errors

### 20. Confidence-Performance Gap
**Researcher:** Overconfidence Effect Literature
**Core Concept:** Miscalibration detection
- Flags gaps > 40%
- **Application:** Immediate dangerous miscalibration detection

### 21. Learning Velocity Analysis
**Researcher:** Learning Analytics Research
**Core Concept:** Progress rate tracking
- Rate of mastery acquisition over time
- **Application:** Predicts time-to-mastery

## Part 3: Modern Frameworks Under Consideration (8 TBD)

Cutting-edge research from 2020-2025 being evaluated for future integration.

### 22. Desirable Difficulties
**Researcher:** Bjork & Bjork (2011-2024)
**Evidence:** d=0.5-0.8 effect sizes
**Core Concept:** Learning conditions that enhance retention
- Interleaving, spacing, retrieval practice, variation
- **Potential Application:** Advanced practice modes

### 23. Productive Failure
**Researcher:** Kapur (2008-2024)
**Evidence:** d=0.68 meta-analytic effect
**Core Concept:** Struggle before instruction
- Activates prior knowledge through exploration
- **Potential Application:** "Explore First" mode

### 24. Self-Determination Theory
**Researcher:** Ryan & Deci (2020 update)
**Evidence:** d=0.49-0.61 across all three needs
**Core Concept:** Intrinsic motivation factors
- Autonomy, Competence, Relatedness
- **Potential Application:** Enhanced choice; peer features

### 25. Embodied Cognition
**Researcher:** Goldin-Meadow (2019-2024)
**Evidence:** d=0.32-0.55
**Core Concept:** Gesture-based learning
- Physical interaction enhances cognition
- **Potential Application:** Interactive gestures; drawing tools

### 26. AI Socratic Tutoring
**Researcher:** Ma et al. (Nature 2025)
**Evidence:** 2x improvement over answer-giving
**Core Concept:** Questioning over telling
- LLM-powered Socratic dialogue
- **Potential Application:** Structured questioning patterns

### 27. Attention Contagion
**Researcher:** Stojic et al. (2024)
**Evidence:** 15-25% performance impact
**Core Concept:** Focus spreads between learners
- Distraction is contagious
- **Potential Application:** Group study recommendations

### 28. Microbreaks for Attention
**Researcher:** 2024-2025 Studies
**Evidence:** 76% better post-test performance
**Core Concept:** Short, frequent breaks
- 60-90 seconds every 10-15 minutes
- **Potential Application:** Built-in session breaks

### 29. Handwriting vs. Typing
**Researcher:** van der Meer (2024-2025)
**Evidence:** 25-40% better recall; EEG theta evidence
**Core Concept:** Handwriting activates deeper encoding
- Motor engagement enhances memory
- **Potential Application:** Handwriting input; sketching tools
